Logitech Mouse

Overview

The Logitech Mouse is a high-quality, durable input device designed for smooth performance. This guide provides step-by-step instructions for dismantling, understanding its components, and identifying recyclable and reusable parts.

Steps to Dismantle

  1. Unplug the mouse from your computer.
  2. Remove the screws located under the mouse feet or stickers using a precision screwdriver.
  3. Carefully separate the top and bottom shells using a prying tool to avoid damaging the plastic.
  4. Disconnect the internal wiring connectors linking the PCB (Printed Circuit Board) to the buttons and scroll wheel.
  5. Remove the PCB and set aside the optical sensor and wiring.

Components

  • Plastic Shell (Top and Bottom)
  • Scroll Wheel Assembly
  • PCB with Optical Sensor and Microcontrollers
  • USB Cable and Connectors
  • Micro Switches (Left and Right Buttons)
  • LED Indicators

Recycling and Reuse

The following components can be recycled or reused:

  • Plastic Shell: Recyclable at e-waste recycling centers. Ensure the plastic is clean and free of adhesive residues.
  • PCB and Optical Sensor: E-waste recycling centers can process the PCB for metals and electronic components.
  • USB Cable: Reusable for DIY projects or recycled for copper extraction.
  • Micro Switches: Can be reused in other electronic projects if functioning properly.
  • Scroll Wheel: Recyclable plastic.
